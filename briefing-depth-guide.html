The Rule for Claude

Layers 1–2 are the system. Same card structure, same anatomy, same font sizes, same padding, same radial glow — only the accent colour and content change. The briefing card can have more or fewer sentences depending on how much the tab has to say; that's natural. Calendar will typically be the longest because it's aggregating across life categories. Shopping and Exercise are single-domain and will be shorter. That's fine — the headline anchors the visual consistency, and the card shape makes them feel like siblings.

Layers 3–5 are tab-specific. The Calendar's week progress strip, highlights carousel, and weather are unique to that tab — don't try to replicate them on Shopping or Exercise. Those tabs already have their own equivalent (horizontal day cards) which serve the same purpose in their own way. The visual consistency lives at the top of the page. Below the hero, each tab is free to do whatever makes sense for its domain.
